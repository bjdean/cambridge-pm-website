And various cam.pm types did actually make their way there, resulting in
at least one bit of Perl:

#!/bin/sh
perl -le'$"="";$;="@ARGV";@_=map{$;=~s;$_;;gi||0}qw(l o v e s);do{@_=split"",
"@_";$_[$_]=$_[$_]+$_[$_+1]foreach 0..@_-2;$#_--}while("@_">100);print "@_%"' $*

Exactly what that does may require some explanation... do you remember
being at primary school and trying to work out how much person X loves
person Y using various scientific methods? The above will use the
following well-known[1] method:

* Write down both names (eg `Philip Kendall' and `his ego')
* Write down (in order) the number of `l's, `o's, `v's, `e's and `s's in
  both words:             [ 3 1 0 2 1 ]
* Add up adjacent digits: [  4 1 2 3  ]
* Repeat                  [   5 3 5   ]
                          [    8 8    ]
* Treat as a percentage => 88%

(If the sum of two digits is greater than 10, just treat the two digits
 as two separate entries in the array. This can cause the the procedure
 not to converge...)

[1] FSVO `well-known'.
