So, it appears (touch wood) that develooper.com have finally fixed their
nameservers so that they're returning the correct replies for the pm.org
domains, and we now have our list back.

For anyone who wondered where it was going, the technical explanation
follows:
develooper.com (who are the authority for the pm.org domain) run 
MyDNS (http://mydns.bboy.net/) on ns, ns2 and ns3.develooper.com. For
some reason (to do with the software they were running), the answers
they returned for records in a subzone had a header suggesting that
authority existed, but didn't actually return any authority RRs. Thus
most nameservers querying it would have given up and returned SERVFAIL
at that point. If you queried the NS records directly, you'd get a correct
answer, and your nameserver would then correctly cache the answer, and
you'd be able to get the answer directly.
