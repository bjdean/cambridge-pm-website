On Friday 05 October 2001 10:36 am, the mighty poet Philip Kendall said 
on the wire:
"" Isn't that more a memory issue than a speed issue? I interpreted
 Tim's "" problem as being a speed one -- he was trying to avoid
 copying every "" array element of every value of %$before.
""

Hmm, you may have a point.

However, if the 'before' and 'after' data structures can't be changed, 
then I can't,  off the top of my head , think of an obvious way round 
this.

If they *can*, I'd be inclined to try 'stringifying' the data in the 
array ref, or encode it with pack(), c.f. Guttman-Rosler 
Transformation.  On the ouput side, this lets you use some combination 
of join, split and/or substr ( or unpack ).  My gut instinct is that 
this would by quicker than the array-copying solution we currently 
have.  If I get a minute, I'll do some benchmarking.

OTOH, I'm inclined to quote Abigail on clpm: 'If speed is important, 
why are are you using Perl?'
