> Anyway, should a semantic error like this (on Debian woody's perl) really
> give me a SEGV? Is there a good reason why the parser can't say "Error,
> you have transfunged the first two arguments?"

It can, you just have to ask it to :)

pan:~$ perl -w -e 'use strict; read( $x, X, 10 )'
Global symbol "$x" requires explicit package name at -e line 1.
Bareword "X" not allowed while "strict subs" in use at -e line 1.
Execution of -e aborted due to compilation errors.

Presumably it SEGVs in your case because it regards filehandles as just a
funny kind of scalar, and tries to write into the memory referenced by it.
I thought it might be converting the bareword X into a string, but I don't
think that's what's happening.
