Having done my training with the Tom Christiansen Perl Consultancy, I 
suppose I have been trained to use the correct way. I was told that 
whilst for and foreach are actually interchangeable because of some C 
programmers wishes, it was bad form to do so. Also, having been a 
teacher, and expecting students to do as told, rather than what they 
want, I always do as told, and so always use

for ($i = 0; $i < @array; $i++) {
	do something to $array[$i];
}

and

foreach (sort keys%hash) {
	do something to $_;
}

I suppose each to their own.
