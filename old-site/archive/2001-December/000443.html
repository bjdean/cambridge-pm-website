On Wed, Dec 05, 2001 at 04:17:17PM +0000, Philip Kendall wrote:
> On Wed, Dec 05, 2001 at 02:19:58PM +0000, Lucy McWilliam wrote:
> > "Know of any comprehensive descriptions of when Perl uses integers to
> > represent numbers, when it uses floats,
> Internally, Perl represents all numbers as floats. Perl 6 will have
> native integer types.

I know it's your birthday, but that's just plain bollocks! :-)

man perlguts and look for SvIV and SvNV.

> > how to get between them
> int(...) will truncate a number towards zero.

Will turn it into an SV with a set SvIOK.

> POSIX::floor and POSIX::ceil give you (respectively) the integers less
> than or equal to and greater than or equal to a number.

Yes, and they return something which will set SvIOK.

> > and what various operators (eg bitwise) do in each case?

Bitwise ops will only work on SvIV parts. So an SvNV part will be cast
to an integer (and the SV will become SvIOK).

Perl's internal representations of things are really not for the
faint-hearted and the way the string and atoi() etc magic are done is
absolutely horrid.

> Generally, The Right Thing(tm). Those operators for which a fractional
> part makes no sense will have it removed.

And the SV SvNOK flag unset, and the SvIOK flag set.
