On Wed, Aug 15, 2001 at 05:18:49AM +0100, Dan Sheppard wrote:
> I've got a Perl script that needs to parse a LALR(1) language. In C I'd use
> lex and yacc, what's the best way to do this in Perl? I realise that there
> are other weird parsing methods more suited to perl (like bag grammars, and
> so on) but it would be nice if there was something that would just take a
> BNF grammar and do the right thing. Is there something people can
> recommend in the standard distribution or on CPAN?

There isn't, as far as I know, an LALR parser out of the box, however, if
you have a BNF that could be rewritten to Recursive Descent instead, there
is Parse::RecDescent by the complete nutcase^W^W^WDamian Conway. That's on
CPAN. I'm not aware of any LALR(1) equivalent, though.

http://search.cpan.org/ may or may not be your friend.

:-)

Beware that the code on CPAN is of *very* variable quality, you may want to
have a quick look at the code for things before actually linking them in...
