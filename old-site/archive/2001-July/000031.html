On Mon, 16 Jul 2001, Matthew Byng-Maddick wrote:

> I'm not sure that I follow what you mean here. The OO idea is that the
> object methods act on *this object*. So bless() and the -> syntax is all
> you need for OO programming. Of course, "real" OO languages provide ways
> to keep some of the internals of an object class private (and this is
> actually possible in Perl, though slightly horrid syntactically[1]), and
> Perl doesn't make this easy.

And many "real" OO languages provide strong static typechecking, which I
think is what Martin wants. Unfortunately static tyepchecking is anathema
to Perl..

As MBM has managed to show, you can almost get this sort of thing with
closures, but it's hard to get right and not a "natural" syntactic
idiom. I keep meaning to look more at CLOS (Common Lisp Object System), as
it seems to do the right stuff.

Let's think of a suitable idiom and work out the implementation later :)
